Additional Resources for First-Year Parents

Navigating the first year of parenthood can be overwhelming, but you're not alone! Here are some helpful resources to provide support and information:

General Parenting Resources

  • HealthyChildren.org (American Academy of Pediatrics): Comprehensive information on child health and development from birth through adolescence.
  • CDC's Learn the Signs. Act Early.: Information on developmental milestones and resources for early intervention.
  • Zero to Three: Resources focused on the development of infants and toddlers.

Postpartum Support

  • Postpartum Support International (PSI): Resources and support for women and families experiencing postpartum depression and anxiety.
  • Mayo Clinic - Postpartum Depression: Information on the symptoms, causes, and treatment of postpartum depression.

Breastfeeding and Infant Feeding

  • La Leche League International: Support and information for breastfeeding mothers.
  • WIC (Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children): Provides food assistance and nutrition education for low-income pregnant women, new mothers, and young children.

Sleep and Safe Sleep

  • National Institute of Child Health and Human Development (NICHD) Safe to Sleep Campaign: Information on safe sleep practices to reduce the risk of SIDS.
  • HealthyChildren.org - Baby Sleep: Articles and advice on infant sleep.

Local Resources

For local support groups, parenting classes, and other community resources, contact your local hospital, health department, or community center.
